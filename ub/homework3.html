Homework 3 - Critique

Critique is a central part of being a good web designer. Critique helps you find ways to improve both the content and design of a web site, so that content is easier to understand and more engaging.

For this homework, I want you to get feedback on your interest narrative from at least one person that knows about your experiences with computing:

  • Show the person your page and have them read it
  • Ask them to: 1) ask questions about the content and 2) provide additional details you may have forgotten

Write down these these questions, details, and improvements. You'll use this as a list of improvements to work on in class tomorrow.

What to Submit

Submit one page that includes:

  1. A description of who you had critique your page
  2. A list of the questions or missing details they identified

This list can be handwritten or printed. Include your name on your submission.

Grading (10 points)

There is no wrong answer for this homework, so you'll get 10 points for submitting. However:

  • If you don't describe who you got feedback from, you'll lose 3 points.
  • If you don't include a list of questions and details, you'll lose 7 points.
